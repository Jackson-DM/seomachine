## Rewrite Guidance (for Super Variant)
- Strong hooks to reuse:
  - Variant 3's contrarian angle ("Brand consistency might be overrated")
  - Variant 5's relatable problem ("Your slides are done. But something feels off.")
  - Variant 9's audience qualifier ("If you're creating slides for X—read this.")
- Framing to keep:
  - Variant 5's step-by-step workflow structure
  - Variant 4's clean tool comparison with specific details
  - Variant 3's honest tradeoff framing (scale vs. polish)
- Language to avoid:
  - "serious edge," "evolving fast," "stay ahead" (hype/salesy)
  - "I just learned something useful" (vague, weak)
  - "The landscape is changing fast" (generic prediction)
  - Any 6-12 month forecasts or "AI will change everything" energy
- Suggested direction for final rewrite:
  - Lead with Variant 5's relatable problem hook or Variant 3's contrarian take.
  - Body: Combine the actionable workflow from Variant 5 (score your slides on 7 criteria) with Variant 4's clean tool comparison. Keep it tight — one workflow + one comparison table, not both at full length.
  - Close with a CTA that gives a reason to engage: reference the next meetup, invite a comment ("what tool are you using?"), or point to a specific resource.
  - Tone: Practitioner sharing a real lesson. Not a summary. Not a listicle. Earned, specific, honest.
