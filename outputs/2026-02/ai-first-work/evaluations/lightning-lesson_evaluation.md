# LinkedIn Post Evaluation

## Context
- Brand: AI-First Work
- ICP: AI-First Work – Career Builder / Upskiller
- Source Content: Content Pillar – Choosing the Right AI Slide Tool
- Date Evaluated: 2026-02-03

---

## Overall Observation

All 10 variants share the same CTA ("Follow AI-First Work for more practical AI guidance") and none name specific tools. The posts are more conceptual than tactical — many repeat the same core point ("tool selection > prompting," "test in parallel," "it's a career skill") with different hooks. The strongest variants provide clear mental models or actionable steps. The weakest ones restate the thesis without earning it.

---

## Post Evaluations

### Post Variant 1 – Career Insight Hook
**Total Score:** 42/70

- Hook: 7 – "The AI skill that actually gets you noticed at work? It's not prompt engineering." Good pattern interrupt, calm and confident. But "gets you noticed" is slightly influencer-adjacent.
- Clarity: 7 – Clear message, easy to follow. Slightly generic in the middle.
- Specificity: 5 – Mentions speed, polish, repeatability, scale — but no tool names or concrete examples. Abstract.
- ICP Relevance: 7 – Career framing works. "Ship faster and look more capable" aligns with career goals.
- Brand Voice: 6 – Mostly good, but "Start building it now" is slightly pushy for this ICP.
- Credibility: 5 – Sounds reasonable but lacks concrete proof or examples. Nothing to anchor trust.
- CTA Fit: 5 – Generic. Same CTA across all 10 variants with no variation or reason to act.

**Why this scored this way:**
- Good hook but the body doesn't deliver enough substance to back it up. Reads like a thesis statement without evidence.

**How to improve:**
- Name at least one specific tool or scenario to ground the advice.
- Soften "Start building it now" — the ICP doesn't respond to urgency.

## Ranking Summary

1. **Top Candidate:** Post Variant 4 (53/70)
   - Why it won: Best ICP alignment in the entire set. This ICP explicitly values "clear mental models" — and this post delivers exactly that with a numbered framework. Clean structure, calm tone, actionable steps. The only variant that gives the reader a reusable tool they can apply immediately.

2. **Second Best:** Post Variant 8 (50/70)
   - Why it performed well: Most actionable post in the set. "Test them against each other" is a concrete, low-barrier action. The tone perfectly matches the ICP — encouraging without pushing. "Try it this week" is a better CTA than the actual CTA line.

3. **Third Best:** Post Variant 3 (49/70)
   - Why it's usable: Strongest voice alignment. The "mistake" framing is authoritative without being fear-based. "More friction than doing it manually" is the most honest, earned line across all 10 variants. Needs more specificity to compete with #1.

## Rewrite Guidance (for Super Variant)
- Strong hooks to reuse:
  - Variant 4's "A simple framework" — directly matches ICP's desire for mental models
  - Variant 3's "A mistake I see professionals make" — calm authority
  - Variant 8's "Test them against each other" — actionable and clear
- Framing to keep:
  - Variant 4's numbered framework structure
  - Variant 8's learn-by-doing approach with a specific time claim ("ten minutes")
  - Variant 3's "more friction than doing it manually" — most honest line in the set
  - The transferable skill angle ("works beyond slides") — but only once, not repeated
- Language to avoid:
  - "Level up" (Variant 2) — LinkedIn boilerplate
  - "High-output professionals" (Variant 6) — influencer framing, alienates ICP
  - "Leverage" and "most people miss" (Variant 9) — overused LinkedIn cliches
  - "Ships while others are still tweaking" (Variant 7) — competitive framing the ICP doesn't want
  - "Start building it now" (Variant 1) — too urgent for this audience
  - Repeating the same CTA verbatim across variants — needs variation and a reason to act
- Suggested direction for final rewrite:
  - Lead with Variant 4's framework hook or Variant 3's mistake hook.
  - Body: Combine Variant 4's numbered decision framework with Variant 8's "test in parallel" as step 3. Add one concrete scenario (e.g., "needed a quick internal deck — used the fastest tool, done in 10 minutes" vs. "client presentation — used the polished tool").
  - Close with Variant 8's "Try it this week" as the CTA — it's calm, actionable, and matches the ICP better than "Follow AI-First Work."
  - Critical gap to fill: None of the 10 variants name a specific tool. The Super Variant should name at least 2-3 tools to ground the advice and build credibility. Abstract advice about "tool selection" needs concrete anchors.
  - Tone: Calm instructor helping someone build a skill. Not a thought leader broadcasting insights. Teach, don't flex.
